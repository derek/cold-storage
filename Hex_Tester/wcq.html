In this Java version, I made it so you can put in your own skill levels and amount of home field advantage each team has.

You might ask if the skill and advantage levels are on a 1-10 scale or a 1-20 scale or whatever, but actually they aren't on any scale. 

They are just relative to each other, meaning that if you think the US is twice as good as Mexico (which we all know they are), the skill levels could be set at

            US skill - 10

            Mexico skill - 5

or it could even be

            US skill - 84

            Mexico skill - 42

But then keep in mind that if you put the levels that high, then you'll have to raise every one else's as well.

 

And the home field advantage works just by adding on whatever # is in the Home Field Advantage box onto the teams skill, but only when they play at home.

So you can look at it like Mexico's skill might be 10 when they play on the road, but with the home field advantage factor when they play at home,

they play like they are a team of a skill level of 15 if their advtantage # is 5.

Got it?

 

And I have entered in the real results of the games already played, so you can choose to use those or not by checking or un-checking the box.

 

And for all you geeks out there who want to know how this works? It's not too complicated. All the program does is use odds, and a 

random number to spit out the results. 

- For each game, the program generates a total number which is based off of 

                TOTAL = (HOME_TEAM_SKILL + HOME_TEAM_ADVANTAGE + AWAY_TEAM_SKILL + TIE_CHANCE)

 

- And tieChance is equal to 

                (HOME_TEAM_SKILL + HOME_TEAM_ADVANTAGE + AWAY_TEAM_SKILL) / 5

That takes in account that there is a 1/5 chance the game will end in a tie. That's the number I came up with from looking at previous WCQ games.

 

- Then a random number is generated who's minimum is 1 and maximum is TOTAL.

 

- If the number is less than 

                (HOME_TEAM_SKILL + HOME_TEAM_ADVANTAGE)

the home team wins.

 

- If the number is greater than 

                (HOME_TEAM_SKILL + HOME_TEAM_ADVANTAGE) 

and less than 

                (HOME_TEAM_SKILL + HOME_TEAM_ADVANTAGE + AWAY_TEAM_SKILL)

then the away team wins

 

- And if the number is anything other than than it's a tie.

 

For example we'll take US @ Mexico.

US Skill = 8

Mexico Skill = 9

Mexico home advantage = 15

TieChance  = 6

So the total number is 38

 

Mexico wins if the random number is in between 0 and 24

US Wins if the random number is in between 25 and 32

It's a Tie if the random number is in between 33 and 38

So it's breaks down to Mexico having a 64% chance at winning, US having a 20% chance, and a tie having a 16% chance at happening.
